# Charter Purpose

The Engineering Operating System (EOS) Charter establishes the guiding philosophy, authority, principles, and operating vision for the Team LIPS Engineering Operating System.

The Charter serves as the constitutional preface to the Engineering Operating System.

It defines **why the EOS exists**, **what it governs**, and **the principles that every Engineering Standard shall follow**.

Operational implementation details are defined within the individual EOS Standards.

---

# Purpose

The Team LIPS Engineering Operating System establishes the standardized engineering framework for designing, governing, implementing, verifying, and continuously improving Team LIPS products.

The EOS exists to provide a repeatable engineering operating model that preserves governance, improves engineering quality, strengthens collaboration, and enables responsible AI-assisted engineering.

The EOS is an engineering framework.

It is not an organizational constitution.

It derives its authority from the approved Team LIPS Governance Foundation.

---

# Authority

The Engineering Operating System operates under the authority of:

1. Lighthouse Constitution
2. Smart Business Constitution (where applicable)
3. Approved Governance Frameworks
4. Founder Decisions
5. Mission Control Governance

Nothing within the Engineering Operating System supersedes these governing authorities.

# Vision

Create an AI-native Engineering Operating System where humans and AI collaborate through governed engineering artifacts rather than conversational memory.

Engineering knowledge becomes portable.

Engineering governance becomes explicit.

Engineering quality becomes measurable.

Engineering history becomes permanent.

# Engineering Philosophy

The Engineering Operating System is founded upon the following principles.

## Humans Serving Humans

Technology exists to improve human capability.

Engineering ultimately serves people rather than technology.

---

## AI Assistant, Not AI Judge

Artificial Intelligence assists engineering.

Humans remain responsible for every engineering decision, governance decision, and production authorization.

---

## Evidence Over Assumptions

Engineering conclusions shall be supported by observable evidence.

Assumptions shall be explicitly identified.

Verification precedes acceptance.

---

## Governance Before Implementation

Implementation follows governance.

No engineering work bypasses the approved governance process.

---

## Artifacts Over Conversations

Engineering conversations generate ideas.

Engineering artifacts preserve engineering knowledge.

Approved engineering artifacts become the permanent engineering record.

---

## GitHub as the Immutable Engineering Record

GitHub preserves approved engineering history.

Repository publication establishes the official engineering baseline.

---

## Engineering Artifact Hub as the Collaboration Layer

The Engineering Artifact Hub provides the shared collaborative workspace for engineering activities.

Engineering collaboration occurs through governed engineering artifacts rather than isolated conversations.

---

## Platform Independence

The Engineering Operating System governs engineering practices rather than engineering technologies.

Platforms may evolve.

Engineering governance remains stable.

---

## Continuous Improvement

The Engineering Operating System evolves through controlled, evidence-based improvement while preserving backward compatibility, engineering history, and governance integrity.

# Purpose

This glossary establishes the standardized engineering vocabulary used throughout the Team LIPS Engineering Operating System (EOS).

Definitions within this glossary apply to all EOS Standards unless explicitly overridden by an approved Engineering Standard.

The glossary exists to promote consistency, reduce ambiguity, and improve communication across engineering, governance, implementation, and AI-assisted workflows.

---

# Glossary Usage

This glossary is an informative reference.

It defines common engineering terminology used throughout the Engineering Operating System.

Engineering governance requirements remain defined by the applicable EOS Standards.

---

# Engineering Terms

## Artifact

A governed engineering asset produced during the engineering lifecycle.

Examples include:

- Architecture
- Requirements
- Prompt
- SQL
- Source Code
- API Specification
- Verification Report
- Diagram
- Decision Record

---

## Engineering Artifact

An engineering work product that possesses a permanent identity, defined ownership, governance status, lifecycle, and traceability.

Engineering Artifacts form the primary unit of collaboration within the Engineering Operating System.

---

## Engineering Artifact Hub

The collaborative engineering workspace where engineering artifacts are created, reviewed, refined, and governed prior to publication.

The Engineering Artifact Hub is defined by EOS-002.

---

## Engineering Artifact Manifest

The structured identity and traceability record associated with every governed engineering artifact.

Defined by EOS-005.

---

## Engineering Workflow

The governed sequence through which engineering work progresses from idea to operational acceptance.

Defined by EOS-008.

---

## Founder Master Archive

The highest-authority engineering archive maintained by the Founder.

The Founder Master Archive preserves approved engineering history independently of any individual engineering platform or repository.

Defined by EOS-014.

---

## Publication Repository

The immutable engineering repository containing approved engineering artifacts.

For Team LIPS, the Publication Repository is GitHub.

Defined by EOS-014.

---

## AI Operational Workspace

An approved AI engineering environment used for planning, engineering production, review, or operational coordination.

Examples include:

- ChatGPT Work
- Claude Project

AI Operational Workspaces assist engineering.

They do not replace engineering governance.

Defined by EOS-014.

---

## Governance

The process through which engineering decisions are reviewed, authorized, and controlled.

Governance determines engineering authority.

It is independent of platform capability.

---

## Mission Control

The engineering governance function responsible for coordinating engineering reviews, maintaining engineering standards, preserving governance, and managing the Engineering Operating System.

Mission Control governs engineering.

Mission Control does not replace Founder authority.

---

## Founder

The strategic authority responsible for constitutional decisions, strategic direction, and production-impacting approvals.

---

## Engineering Review

The technical evaluation of an engineering artifact.

Engineering Review evaluates engineering quality.

It does not provide governance approval.

---

## Governance Review

Mission Control evaluation of engineering readiness, governance compliance, lifecycle progression, and architectural consistency.

---

## Verification

Evidence-based confirmation that implementation matches the approved engineering artifact.

Verification answers:

> Did we build it correctly?

---

## Validation

Confirmation that the implemented solution satisfies the intended engineering objective.

Validation answers:

> Did we build the correct solution?

---

## Operational Readiness

The determination that an engineering capability is suitable for operational use based on governance, implementation, verification, and evidence.

---

## Engineering Quality

The degree to which engineering work satisfies approved Team LIPS Engineering Standards.

---

## Calibration

Evidence-based verification that engineering platforms, workflows, governance processes, or implementation environments continue to operate according to approved standards.

---

## Traceability

The ability to connect engineering artifacts, decisions, implementations, evidence, and operational history throughout the engineering lifecycle.

---

## Product Truth

The approved definition of product behaviour, business rules, and operational expectations.

Product Truth governs implementation.

---

## Immutable Repository

The authoritative repository containing approved engineering artifacts and engineering history.

For Team LIPS, this repository is GitHub.

---

## AI Platform

An approved artificial intelligence system that assists engineering activities while remaining subject to human governance.

Examples include:

- ChatGPT
- Claude

---

## Capability

A function that a platform is technically able to perform.

Capability does not imply governance authority.

---

## Authority

The approved governance responsibility to authorize engineering actions.

Authority is determined by governance rather than platform permissions.

---

## Engineering Record

A permanent operational record describing an engineering event, issue, decision, observation, lesson learned, or operational intelligence.

Engineering Records are governed by EOS-013.

# Purpose

The EOS Version Compatibility Policy establishes the principles governing the controlled evolution of the Team LIPS Engineering Operating System.

The policy ensures that the Engineering Operating System evolves while preserving engineering history, governance integrity, engineering traceability, and long-term maintainability.

Engineering standards shall evolve in a predictable, transparent, and traceable manner.

---

# Scope

This policy governs the evolution of the **Engineering Operating System (EOS)** itself.

It applies to:

- EOS Charter
- Foundational References
- Engineering Standards
- Engineering Operating System documentation
- Engineering governance standards

This policy does **not** govern compatibility for:

- Software APIs
- Programming libraries
- Application source code
- Database schemas
- Runtime platforms

Software compatibility shall be governed by the respective engineering artifacts and implementation standards.

---

# Objectives

This policy exists to:

- Standardize EOS versioning.
- Preserve backward compatibility.
- Control breaking changes.
- Protect engineering history.
- Maintain engineering traceability.
- Enable continuous improvement.
- Provide predictable upgrade paths.
- Preserve long-term organizational knowledge.

---

# Versioning Philosophy

The Engineering Operating System evolves through controlled versioning.

Version numbers communicate the engineering significance of a change rather than the size of the document.

Every published EOS version shall remain permanently identifiable, searchable, and traceable.

---

# EOS Version vs Document Version

The Engineering Operating System distinguishes between:

## EOS Version

Represents the official release of the Engineering Operating System.

Examples:

```
v1.0.0  
v1.1.0  
v2.0.0  
```

---

## Document Revision

Represents the working state of an individual engineering artifact before publication.

Examples:

```
Draft

Approved (RC1)

Approved (RC2)

Approved  
```

Document revisions do not change the EOS Version.

---

# Version Number Format

The Engineering Operating System follows Semantic Versioning.

```
Major.Minor.Patch  
```

Examples

```
v1.0.0  
v1.1.0  
v1.2.3  
v2.0.0  
```

---

# Version Definitions

## Major Version

Major versions introduce significant architectural or governance evolution.

Examples include:

- Engineering architecture redesign
- Governance restructuring
- Lifecycle redesign
- Breaking engineering changes
- Major workflow redesign
- Fundamental EOS evolution

Example

```
v1.x.x → v2.0.0  
```

Major versions require:

- Founder approval
- Mission Control review
- Migration guidance
- Updated compatibility documentation

---

## Minor Version

Minor versions introduce compatible improvements without disrupting existing engineering practices.

Examples include:

- New Engineering Standards
- Additional Foundational References
- Expanded engineering workflows
- New governance capabilities
- Documentation improvements

Example

```
v1.0.0 → v1.1.0  
```

Minor versions preserve compatibility with previous releases.

---

## Patch Version

Patch versions correct editorial or operational issues.

Examples include:

- Editorial corrections
- Clarified wording
- Diagram improvements
- Cross-reference updates
- Typographical corrections

Example

```
v1.0.0 → v1.0.1  
```

Patch releases shall never modify engineering intent or governance authority.

---

# Compatibility Commitment

Every EOS release should preserve:

- Engineering artifacts
- Governance history
- Engineering traceability
- Artifact identifiers
- Lifecycle history
- Decision records
- Verification history
- Engineering knowledge

Backward compatibility shall be preserved whenever practical.

Breaking changes shall remain exceptional.

---

# Breaking Changes

A breaking change occurs when an EOS revision requires modification of previously approved engineering practices.

Examples include:

- Artifact lifecycle redesign
- Governance authority changes
- Engineering Artifact Manifest restructuring
- Decision authority restructuring
- Workflow redesign
- Engineering terminology changes affecting governance

Breaking changes require:

- Major version increment
- Migration guidance
- Mission Control approval
- Founder approval

---

# Non-Breaking Changes

Examples include:

- Editorial improvements
- Clarified wording
- Additional examples
- New diagrams
- Additional references
- Cross-reference improvements
- New implementation guidance

These changes may be released as Minor or Patch versions.

---

# Deprecation Policy

Engineering standards shall not be removed immediately.

Instead they shall progress through the following lifecycle.

```
Current

↓

Deprecated

↓

Superseded

↓

Archived  
```

Deprecated standards remain valid until officially retired.

---

# Superseded Standards

When an engineering standard is replaced:

- Original versions remain preserved.
- Replacement standards reference previous versions.
- Engineering history remains intact.
- Traceability shall never be lost.

Superseded standards remain part of the permanent engineering record.

---

# Migration Policy

Major EOS revisions shall include:

- Migration guidance
- Compatibility notes
- Deprecated standards
- Replacement standards
- Required migration actions
- Optional improvements
- Transition recommendations

Migration shall prioritize engineering continuity and governance stability.

# Executive Summary

The Team LIPS Engineering Operating System (EOS) establishes the permanent architectural framework governing how humans, AI engineering environments, engineering repositories, and implementation platforms collaborate through governed engineering artifacts.

The Engineering Operating System is not a software platform.

It is the engineering operating model used to govern engineering activities across current and future Team LIPS products.

The Engineering Operating System replaces conversation-centric engineering with artifact-centric engineering, ensuring that engineering knowledge is preserved through governed engineering artifacts rather than transient conversational context.

The Engineering Operating System is designed to:

- Reduce manual copy/paste.
- Reduce duplicated engineering work.
- Reduce conversational dependency.
- Improve engineering traceability.
- Improve AI collaboration.
- Preserve Founder authority.
- Preserve Mission Control governance.
- Maintain GitHub as the immutable engineering repository.
- Establish the Engineering Artifact Hub as the collaborative engineering workspace.
- Remain reusable across future Team LIPS products.

---

# Purpose

EOS-001 establishes the executive architecture of the Team LIPS Engineering Operating System.

It provides the high-level architectural view from which all subsequent EOS standards derive.

Detailed governance, workflows, lifecycle rules, metadata standards, and operational procedures are defined within their respective EOS standards.

---

# Vision

Engineering knowledge should exist independently of any individual AI conversation or engineering platform.

Engineering artifacts—not conversations—become the primary unit of engineering collaboration.

Every engineering decision shall be represented by governed engineering artifacts possessing permanent identity, ownership, lifecycle, and traceability.

---

# Architecture Principles

The Engineering Operating System follows the principles established in the **Engineering Operating System Charter**.

Its architectural design is based upon:

- Humans remain responsible for engineering decisions.
- AI assists engineering but does not govern it.
- Engineering artifacts become the permanent engineering record.
- Governance precedes implementation.
- Engineering knowledge remains platform-independent.
- Engineering history remains traceable.
- Continuous improvement is evidence-driven.

---

The Engineering Operating System separates governance from engineering execution.

Governance directs engineering.

Engineering produces governed artifacts.

Implementation consumes approved engineering artifacts.

# Executive Summary

The Engineering Capability & Authority Taxonomy establishes the common vocabulary used to describe engineering capabilities across approved Team LIPS platforms, workflows, and engineering roles.

The taxonomy intentionally separates:

- Technical capability
- Operational responsibility
- Governance authority
- Operational readiness
- Evidence requirements

This separation ensures that technical capability never creates governance authority.

A platform may be technically capable of performing an action while remaining unauthorized to perform it under Team LIPS governance.

---

# Purpose

The Engineering Capability & Authority Taxonomy exists to:

- Standardize engineering terminology.
- Classify platform capabilities consistently.
- Preserve governance boundaries.
- Support engineering planning.
- Improve capability comparison.
- Improve engineering traceability.
- Support evidence-based platform verification.
- Provide a reusable vocabulary for current and future Team LIPS platforms.
- Support future automation under explicit governance.

---

# Taxonomy Principles

The taxonomy follows the principles established by the Engineering Operating System Charter.

## 1. Capability Does Not Equal Authority

A platform may technically perform an operation.

Technical capability does not authorize execution.

---

## 2. Governance Determines Permission

Governance determines whether, when, and by whom an engineering action may occur.

Platform permissions only expose technical capability.

---

## 3. Responsibility Does Not Equal Approval

A role may be responsible for preparing or executing engineering work without possessing approval authority.

Engineering responsibility and governance authority remain separate.

---

## 4. Evidence Establishes Readiness

A capability becomes operationally usable only after direct verification and appropriate governance approval.

Vendor documentation or platform descriptions alone do not establish operational readiness.

---

## 5. Platform Independence

Capability definitions remain stable across platforms.

New engineering platforms shall be evaluated using the existing taxonomy rather than creating platform-specific terminology.

---

# Taxonomy Dimensions

Every engineering operation shall be evaluated across five independent dimensions.

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Capability Class | What kind of action is technically possible? |
| Impact Level | What is the potential operational impact? |
| Operational Responsibility | Who prepares or performs the work? |
| Governance Authority | Who may authorize the action? |
| Verification Status | Has the capability been directly verified? |

No single dimension replaces another.

---

# Engineering Capability Classes

Every engineering capability shall be classified using one or more of the following capability classes.

| Capability Class | Definition |
|------------------|------------|
| Observe | View or inspect information without changing system state |
| Analyze | Interpret engineering information and identify meaning, patterns, risks, or implications |
| Generate | Produce a new engineering artifact or proposed engineering output |
| Review | Evaluate engineering work against approved requirements or standards |
| Recommend | Propose an engineering or governance action without authorizing it |
| Approve | Authorize governance progression or a controlled engineering action |
| Implement | Translate approved engineering artifacts into working implementation |
| Modify | Change an engineering artifact, platform configuration, or implementation state |
| Verify | Confirm that implementation matches the approved engineering artifact |
| Validate | Confirm that the implemented solution satisfies the intended objective |
| Deploy | Publish implementation to a runtime or production environment |
| Monitor | Observe runtime health, activity, performance, or operational conditions |
| Integrate | Connect platforms or exchange governed engineering artifacts |
| Administer | Manage platform, workspace, access, organization, or infrastructure configuration |
| Archive | Preserve engineering history after active use |
| Recover | Restore engineering capability, data, artifacts, or operational service after disruption |

An engineering operation may carry more than one capability classification.

For example:

```text
Deploy an Edge Function  
```

may include:

- Implement
- Modify
- Deploy

---

# Capability Impact Levels

Every capability shall also be assigned an impact level.

| Level | Name | Description |
|------:|------|-------------|
| L1 | Observation | No intended change to artifacts, configuration, or runtime |
| L2 | Engineering Production | Creates or changes working engineering artifacts without runtime impact |
| L3 | Implementation | Changes application code, schema, configuration, or implementation assets |
| L4 | Production Impact | Changes customer-facing, production, security-sensitive, or operational systems |
| L5 | Constitutional Impact | Changes permanent Team LIPS engineering governance or authority |

Higher impact levels require progressively stronger governance and evidence.

---

# Governance Authority Classes

Governance authority is independent of technical capability and operational responsibility.

| Authority Class | Description |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Advisory | May provide guidance, analysis, or recommendations only |
| Operational | May perform approved routine engineering work within assigned scope |
| Mission Control | May authorize engineering governance progression and operational acceptance within approved authority |
| Founder | May authorize strategic, production-impacting, and reserved engineering decisions |
| Constitutional | Applies to permanent governance standards requiring the highest approved authority |

The specific decision authority for engineering actions is defined by:

**EOS-007 — Engineering Decision Authority Matrix**

# Security & Access Rules

Engineering access shall follow the principle of least privilege.

Access shall be determined by:

- Operational responsibility
- Governance authority
- Artifact classification
- Engineering role

Platform permissions shall never replace governance authority.

# Version Synchronization Rules

Working versions exist within the Engineering Artifact Hub.

Published versions exist within GitHub.

Historical versions remain within the Founder Master Archive.

Synchronization shall preserve:

- Artifact identity
- Version history
- Traceability
- Governance records
