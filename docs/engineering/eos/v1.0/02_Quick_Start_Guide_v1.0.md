# Welcome

Welcome to the **Team LIPS Engineering Operating System (EOS).**

This guide helps new engineers, Mission Control, AI platforms, and contributors quickly understand how engineering operates within Team LIPS.

The Engineering Operating System is the standard operating model for engineering activities across Smart Business and future Team LIPS products.

Rather than relying on conversation history, engineering knowledge is preserved through governed engineering artifacts, explicit decision-making, and evidence-based verification.

---

# Engineering Philosophy

Before writing code or creating documents, understand the core philosophy.

## Humans Serving Humans

Technology exists to improve human capability.

Engineering ultimately serves people.

---

## AI Assistant, Not AI Judge

Artificial Intelligence assists engineering.

Humans remain responsible for governance, architecture, and production decisions.

---

## Governance Before Implementation

No implementation begins until engineering artifacts have been reviewed and approved.

---

## Evidence Over Assumptions

Engineering decisions are supported by observable evidence.

Verification precedes acceptance.

---

## Artifacts Over Conversations

Conversations generate ideas.

Artifacts preserve engineering knowledge.

Only approved artifacts become part of the permanent engineering record.

# Before You Start Any Engineering Work

Confirm:

- The request has been received.
- Governance is understood.
- Relevant EOS standards have been reviewed.
- Existing artifacts have been checked.
- Similar work has not already been completed.

Never duplicate engineering effort unnecessarily.

---

# Before Publishing

Before publishing any engineering artifact confirm:

- Engineering Review completed.
- Mission Control Review completed.
- Founder approval obtained (where required).
- Verification evidence available.
- Artifact metadata completed.
- Version updated.

Only approved artifacts are published.

---

# Engineering Best Practices

Always:

- Follow governance.
- Reuse existing artifacts.
- Maintain traceability.
- Record engineering decisions.
- Preserve engineering history.
- Use approved templates.
- Document assumptions.
- Collect evidence.

Avoid:

- Bypassing governance.
- Working only in conversations.
- Publishing unapproved work.
- Duplicating engineering artifacts.
- Losing engineering history.
